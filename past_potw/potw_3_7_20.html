Problem of the Week

Week of 3/7 - 3/20

A block with large mass M slides with speed V_0 on a frictionless table toward a wall. It collides elastically with a ball with a small mass m, which is initially at rest at a distance L from the wall. The ball slides toward the wall, bounces elastically, and then proceeds to bounce back and forth between the block and the wall. Assume M >> m

  1. How close does the block come to the wall?
  2. How many times does the ball bounce off the block, by the time the block makes its closest approach to the wall?

Problem of the weeks are problems that will be shown on the SPS website from Saturday morning to Friday night. It is meant to be done at your leisure time and done out of curiosity and not out of obligation. The winner will have a chance for their image and bio to be shown on this website.

If you think you solved the problem, please email ucbsps “at” gmail “dot” com with the subject line “Problem of The Week. Show some work! You can also email us if you have problem suggestions. The email must contain both the problem, steps, and the solution. It must be original, unique, and not easily searchable on the internet.
